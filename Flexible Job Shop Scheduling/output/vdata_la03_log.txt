MODEL
-----
Players are the jobs; each is selfish and minimises its own completion
time C_i (payoff u_i = -C_i). The social objective is the makespan

the makespan, so the makespan-critical job deviates by re-routing or
re-sequencing its critical operations; a deviation is kept only if the
makespan drops. When no critical-path deviation helps, the schedule is
a Nash equilibrium (a critical-path local optimum). Iterated kicks and
many random restarts (keeping the best run) escape weak equilibria.

fictitious-play belief learning + Global/Local greedy seeding +
iterated local search + multiple runs.

players' learned beliefs and greedy heuristics. Each step makes the best
deviation - a single job re-routing/re-sequencing, or two rival jobs that
share a machine swapping order or rerouting together - until neither any

single best run is reported below. A single payoff drives everything.
